Mais, quest-ce que cest?!

What is this mysterious product?
Papier Machine is an immersive learning experience, centered on.an interactive & design-forward toolkit. Our first version — Papier Machine Vol. 0 — features a series of six colorful paper toolkits that can be assembled from scratch.
Now you can learn about the fascinating world of electric circuits from the inside out.

Imagine a world without sound — you could not listen to music or sing in the shower.

Indeed, it's a frightening scenario. In our first installment, we focus on this essential part of our lives. With a special silver ink capable of conducting electricity, a graphite pencil and a marble ball, you'll be able to create your own sound circuits.

Once upon a time, in Paris, Papier Machine was born.

Our team started working Papier Machine in January 2015. In a world dominated by technology, our designers started asking the question What hides inside our day-to-day devices?.

This was the first step into a long, exciting and challenging journey that brought us to today. It was through passion, curiosity and research that we were able to produce our first prototype.

Papier Machine made its first public appearance in 2017, at the Musees des Arts decoratifs in Paris. This was our first chance to present and explain our product to the world. We wanted it to be an interactive experience and allow visitor to try, test and play with Papier Machine. After collecting feedback from our supporters and industry leaders, we got back to work and develop the final product.

We are a three-member team based in Paris.

Raphael Pluvinage and Marion Pinaffo are designers running their own studio, Pinaffo Plunivage. Agnes Agullo is a professional with many years of experience within the music world and charity organisations.

We all met and shared the passion to promote alternative learning. This is the first product in a series that we plan to produce for different experiences with electricity.
